RUSH FAQ

  • What is Rush?

    Rush is a period of time near the beginning of a semester where students can meet members of different Greek organizations and get a glimspse of what Greek life is. Usually lasting 1-2 weeks, various events are held for potentials to get to know the Active House.

  • Should I attend Rush Events?

    If you are serious about joining Lambda Phi Epsilon, you are highly encouraged to attend all rush events from beginning to end. Rush is designed to give interested individuals a glimpse into real fraternity life, so the more you experience and see for yourself, the better idea you will have of whether the fraternity is something you want to be a part of. ΑΦ Chapter requires at least 2 rush events and one informational to be considered for a bid.

  • What happens after Rush?

    If you are interested in joining us, you will be offered an interview. After that, if the brothers feel that you are a good fit for the fraternity, you will be given an invitation to pledge and become a brother of Lambda Phi Epsilon. There is no obligation for you to take action after rush has concluded. Rush is simply an opportunity for you to introduce yourself and become more familiar with the fraternity and the brothers.

  • How will joining Lambda Phi Epsilon affect my education?

    While you are encouraged to learn more about the Greek system, we want you to remember why you are at school in the first place. Be sure to find the right balance to have a rewarding college life both academically and socially.

  • Do I need to be Asian to join?

    No. Lambda Phi Epsilon is an Asian interest fraternity that focuses on the promotion of Asian American awareness, but members of every ethnicity, race, religion, and creed are accepted.
